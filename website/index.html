Step 2 · Learn one idea

The membrane is a selective boundary.

A bacterial cell’s membrane regulates what moves into and out of the cell, helping it maintain the conditions it needs to function.

Step 3 · Recall it

Which structure controls movement into and out of the cell?

Not quite. DNA stores genetic instructions. The cell membrane regulates movement across the cell boundary.
Reveal the answer

Cell membrane. It regulates movement into and out of the cell.

04 / Project truth

Built enough to use. Early enough to shape.

Delivered in v0.1

  • Browser and Tauri desktop learning surfaces
  • Deterministic sessions, evidence, feedback, and recaps
  • Portable pack contracts, validation, archives, and SDK tooling
  • Local persistence and defensive pack installation
  • An openly licensed Logic Foundations example pack

The local-first boundary

  • No accounts
  • No hosted learner state
  • No analytics
  • No application backend
  • Progress and installed packs stay local
